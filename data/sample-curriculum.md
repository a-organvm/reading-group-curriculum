# Foundations of Recursive Systems

*An 8-week exploration of recursive, self-referential, and strange-loop systems — from formal logic through creative practice. Draws on foundational texts and ORGAN-I project artifacts to build intuition for how recursion generates novelty.*

**Theme:** recursive-systems  
**Duration:** 8 weeks  
**Focus:** ORGAN-I  

---

## Week 1: Strange Loops and Tangled Hierarchies

### Readings

- geb-ch1
- geb-ch6

### Discussion Questions

1. What makes a loop 'strange' vs merely circular?
1. How does crossing levels of abstraction generate meaning?
1. Can you identify a strange loop in your own creative practice?

### Activities

- Map a strange loop in a system you know well — draw the levels and the cross-level interaction

---

## Week 2: Self-Reference in Formal Systems

### Readings

- geb-ch13
- recursive-engine-readme

### Discussion Questions

1. How does Gödel's incompleteness theorem relate to creative systems?
1. What does the recursive-engine's generative entity model gain from self-reference?
1. Where does self-reference become pathological rather than productive?

### Activities

- Write a 200-word description of your own work that references itself

## Week 3: Levels of Learning and Meta-Cognition

### Readings

- bateson-ecology-ch4
- cog-init-readme

### Discussion Questions

1. What are Bateson's three levels of learning and why does Level III matter?
1. How does the cognitive-archaeology approach map to Bateson's levels?
1. What would it mean for a system to 'learn to learn to learn'?

### Activities

- Identify one thing you learned this week, then identify what you learned about learning from that experience

## Week 4: Ontological Foundations

### Readings

- organon-noumenon-readme
- radix-recursiva-readme

### Discussion Questions

1. What is the boundary between what can be formalized and what resists formalization?
1. How does the solve-coagula process relate to analysis and synthesis?
1. What does it mean for an ontology to be 'generative'?

### Activities

- Decompose a concept you care about using the solve-coagula pattern: dissolve it into parts, then recombine in a new way

## Week 5: Autopoiesis and Self-Producing Systems

### Readings

- maturana-varela-ch1
- nal-readme

### Discussion Questions

1. What distinguishes an autopoietic system from a merely self-replicating one?
1. How do narratological algorithmic lenses treat narrative as self-producing?

## Week 7: Recursive Art and Generative Practice

- hofstadter-surfaces-ch2

### Discussion Questions

1. What aesthetic qualities emerge from recursive processes that don't emerge from linear ones?
1. How does the ORGAN-I to ORGAN-II pipeline transform theory into art?
